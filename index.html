Humans, throughout history, have adopted many different tokens and forms of exchange. From cowrie shells, to credit ledger systems, to bullion, to paper cash, to bits stored on a computer. Money and debt are perhaps *the* central organizing aspect of modern societies. So much so that hardly anyone knows what it is or where it came from. Adam Smith hypothesised that credit systems superceded barter because it made trade more efficient. This seems reasonable enough, but that's not how it went down historically, the truth is more mysterious. Money, is a quantification of what we owe each other in society. And yet this doesn't sit well with us, morally speaking. There is a great degree of moral confusion surrounding money.
